Index
of Mathematicians (and honorary mathematicians)
This  index lists  the individuals whose mathematics
is celebrated on this site. Theorem of the Day's theorem descriptions    generally include an attempt
to attribute the theorem to specific people and it is these people who are currently included in this index. I would be
very grateful to have inaccuracies brought to my attention (either by emailing me
or writing in the Visitors' Book). If a personal hero of yours is missing from the
list, please suggest a theorem of theirs which I can add to Theorem of the Day, thereby including them here.
Clicking on a theorem will take you to the pdf description of it offered
by this website. Highlighting the theorem will also bring up its entry in the main theorem list where full information about the theorem entry may be found.
Clicking on the name of a mathematician will open a new
window with their biography from, say, MacTutor, Numericana.com or Wikipedia, if one exists; failing
that, you will get their own web page. Where I could find nothing helpful
about them at all to link to you will get instead my list of mathematicians deserving a better web presence ...
The page features pop-up menus, potentially an enemy to accessibility. It will probably work on Android phones but not iPhones. I borrowed the css from Andrey Ciclicci.
Thank you also
to John H. Halton whose
idea this index was.
