itinerary
noun
itin·​er·​ary
ī-ˈti-nə-ˌrer-ē
ə-,
chiefly British nonstandard  -ˈti-nə-rē
plural itineraries
Synonyms of itinerary
1
: the route of a journey or tour or the proposed outline of one
2
: a traveler's guidebook
3
: a travel diary
Examples of itinerary in a Sentence
Progresses could last for up to two months; they usually took place between July and October, and were carefully planned in advance, with the itinerary being set out in detailed tables …

On the Sea Paradise itinerary are several exciting dives each week, including a thrilling, must-do dive with manta rays …

The ancient buildings and monuments of Luxor were high on the itinerary of the first modern tourists to the Levant …
—Robert Fox,                The Inner Sea,                1993

itinerary
adjective
Etymology
Middle English itinerarie "route of a journey," from Latin itinerarium (same meaning), derived from earlier itiner-, iter "journey, passage," from ire "to go"  — related to exit, issue, transit
