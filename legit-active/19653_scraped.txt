He was born 3 April 1924 in Tull, Arkansas. He enlisted as an Aviation Cadet in May 1943, and received orders to report to Active Duty 3 June 1943. He took Basic Training at Miami Beach, Florida, College Training (CTD) at the University of Tulsa; Oklahoma and Navigation at Ellington Field, Texas. In October 1944 he joined the bomb crew (Lt. Ryder) at Charleston AAF, South Carolina. The crew arrived at Bari, Italy in January 1945 where they were assigned to the 738th Squadron. He flew 10 missions with Lt. Ryder, and then was selected for radar training at Bari. On the next mission Lt. Ryder and crew ditched their B-24 in the Adriatic Sea. Five crew members including Lt. Ryder and the officer who took his place lost their lives in the accident. He was discharged in January 1946 and joined the Reserves. The next four years were spent in college at the University of Arkansas and he later returned to active duty in January 1957. He flew 29 missions in the Korean War and served a tour in the Vietnam War. He retired from the Air Force on 3 April 1980 with the rank of Colonel. His decorations included The Legion of Merit, Meritorious Service Medal with OLC and the Air Medal with 4OLC. After retiring from his military career he started a new career as a stock broker from which he retired only recently. He was a member of the R.R.O.G., a retired Officers club at Ft. McPherson. He was an avid golfer and played every week. He was a member of the Red Oak Methodist Church in College Park where he and his wife, Helen, were members for 30 years. Mr. Smith is survived by his four children, Sherry Allyson Smith, Little Rock, Arkansas, Stanley Miles (Dot) Smith, Homewood, Alabama, Sally Anne (John) Liquori, Roswell, Georgia, Scott Anthony (Amy) Smith, Roswell, Georgia; five grandchildren, Jessica Marie De Los Santos, Santa Barbara, California, Kate Adele Venable, Homewood, Alabama, Julia Elaine Venable, Homewood, Alabama, Andrew Miles Liquori, Roswell, Georgia, Kristin Grace Liquori, Roswell, Georgia; brothers, Johnny Basil Smith, Horace Wright Smith and wife Bettie Jean, and one sister-in-law, Lou Ella Smith. He is preceded in death by his loving wife, Helen, brothers, Louie Collie Smith, Robert Dean Smith; sister, Nedith Vivian Smith; sisters-in-law, Iva Jane Smith, and Jewell Gladys Smith. The family will receive friends from 12:00 pm to 2:00 pm on Friday, 9 February 2007, and services honoring Mr. Smith's life will be held at 2:00 pm in the chapel of Holly Hill Funeral Home 431 SW Broad Street Fairburn, Georgia 30213, 770-964-7833, with the Rev. Jerry Yates officiating. Interment will be held at Arlington National Cemetery, Arlington, Virginia on 23 February 2007, at 3:00 pm. Flowers are being accepted or contributions may be made to a favorite charity. Holly Hill Funeral home is honored to serve the Smith family.
