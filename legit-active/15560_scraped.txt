Michael Wildman
Michael Wildman is a British actor who appeared in the film Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix. He played the role of aggressive, feisty centaur Magorian, who plays a small part in the eventual outcome of the plot.
Prior to accepting this role, Michael was in the West End playing Lance Corporal Dawson in A Few Good Men alongside Rob Lowe and other programmes, most notably in Episode 5 of the British sitcom Extras alongside Samuel L. Jackson and in the part of the first Marc MacKenzie in Family Affairs.

About the artist
Michael Wildman
In the Day of Cold Light
(Dix-Sept Femmes Accroupies)
Auguste Rodin is most popularly known for "The Kiss" and "The Thinker", of which at the end of his career it is said he had no real affection for. Amongst his other many but less known sculptures, are the ones he lavished a great deal of time and passion, "Gates of Hell", "The Age of Bronze", "Walking Man", "The Burghers of Calais" and "Crouching Woman" (or the frog as it was known amongst some of Rodin's literary friends).
It is the "Crouching Woman" that inspired this project. I first saw the sculpture at an exhibition of Rodin's work at The Royal Academy, London, in December 2006, and it left a deep impression on me. It has, despite its apparent vulnerability, enormous strength and dignity. Some months later in the summer of 2007 I showed my first crouching woman. This was a studio shot and as such pleased me, but I was pensive....

Michael Wildman
Michael Wildman is a British actor who appeared in the film Harry Potter and the Order of the Phoenix. He played the role of aggressive, feisty centaur Magorian, who plays a small part in the eventual outcome of the plot.
Prior to accepting this role, Michael was in the West End playing Lance Corporal Dawson in A Few Good Men alongside Rob Lowe and other programmes, most notably in Episode 5 of the British sitcom Extras alongside Samuel L. Jackson and in the part of the first Marc MacKenzie in Family Affairs.
