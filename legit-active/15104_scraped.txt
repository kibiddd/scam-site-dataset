Then, after Luis Castillo walked, Hernan Perez and James Robbins struck out against Dayton closer Drew Hayes.The Whitecaps also squandered a scoring opportunity in the eighth when Dixon Machado fanned with the bases loaded.Ryan Hamme trimmed a 3-1 deficit to one run earlier in the inning on a fly ball lost by center fielder Jefry Sierra turned into a run-scoring double.DRAGONS 3, WHITECAPS 2Next game:West Michigan (Josue Carreno 5-9, 5.10) at Dayton (Stalin Gerson 3-1, 3.28), 7 p.m. ThursdayRadio:WBBL-FM (107.3), WHTC-AM (1450), WDEE-FM (97.3)Hamme was ejected moments later after being called out on a controversial play at home plate on a single by Nick Castellanos.The Whitecaps stranded 10 runners, including five in the final two innings, and were 3-for-13 with runners in scoring position.West Michigan fell behind early when reigning Midwest League Pitcher of the Week Kyle Ryan surrendered a solo home run to Juan Duran in the third.The Whitecaps tied it at 1-1 in the fourth inning on a Machado RBI ground out.The Dragons reclaimed the lead with runs in the fourth and fifth innings.Ryan (4-9) was tagged with the loss after allowing three runs on six hits. He struck out five and walked one.Perez had two doubles and scored a run, and Clay Jones added two more hits to a torrid stretch. He is 8-for-11 in his past three games.Castillo singled to left field to lead off the game, extending his hitting streak to a team-high 11 games.The Whitecaps finished with nine hits and tied a season high by drawing eight walks.If you purchase a product or register for an account through a link on our site, we may receive compensation.
