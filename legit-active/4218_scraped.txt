The Fire have never lost a home U.S. Open Cup match after the tournament's round of 16, holding a perfect 11-0-0 record, outscoring opponents 30-10, in quarterfinals, semifinals and finals played in Chicagoland.Tuesday's clash marks the second meeting between the Fire and the USL Pro Side. In the previous meeting, the Fire rode a 56th minute goal from Dipsy Selolwane to defeat the Kickers 1-0 at University of Richmond Stadium and advance to the 2004 U.S. Open Cup Semifinals.Since 1998, the Fire have racked up an impressive 27-8-3 record in Open Cup play, which has helped the squad reach the semifinal round on eight out of 14 occasions and the tournament's final five times. The 2006 title marked the ninth time a team from Chicago won the Open Cup and made the Fire one of five teams to capture the crown at least four times in the tournament's nearly century-long history.Advance tickets for the Fire - Kickers match are available by calling 888-MLS-Fire, via Chicago-Fire.com and Ticketmaster. The U.S. Open Cup tilt is Playoff Game 1 of the Fire Season Ticket package.The Fire have raised the prestigious Dewar Cup on four previous occasions and if the Fire advances it will have the opportunity to tie Bethlehem Steel and Maccabi Los Angeles with five U.S. Open Cup titles apiece.CHICAGO FIRE vs. Richmond Kickers* The Chicago Fire are a combined 6-0-0 in home U.S. Open Cup semifinals and finals, outscoring opponents 14-5 in those matches.* The Fire are 5-2-0 all-time in U.S. Open Cup semifinals, with the club's only blemishes coming in matching 1-0 losses to the LA Galaxy in 2001 and FC Dallas in 2005.* The Richmond Kickers fell to Orlando City 3-0 in the USL Pro Semifinals Saturday evening at the Citrus Bowl in Orlando, Fla. Tuesday's U.S. Open tilt is the last scheduled tilt for the Kickers. With a victory on Tuesday, the Richmond side would face the winner of the Seattle Sounders FC - FC Dallas U.S. Open Cup semifinal.* Dominic Oduro tallied his team-leading ninth goal of the season with his strike in the 17th minute of Saturday's 2-0 win over Colorado. Already having hit a career best for goals in an MLS season, the Ghanaian sits one away from becoming the first Fire player since Damani Ralph in 2004 to score double-digit goals during the regular season.* Pavel Pardo tallied two assists in 45 minutes against the Rapids, while Sebastian Grazzini grabbed an assist on Gibbs' 35th minute header. The Fire's two foreign summer signings have combined for three goals and five assists since the beginning of August.* Fire defender Cory Gibbs tallied just his third career MLS goal as he headed home Grazzini's cross against the team he spent 2008-09 with. Gibbs has scored two of his three MLS goals with the Fire in 2011.* Making four saves in the match, Sean Johnson recorded his sixth shutout of the season and ninth of his MLS career.* With three goals, striker Orr Barouch leads the way in scoring for the Fire in U.S. Open Cup play (qualifying included) this year. The on-loan striker scored in the club's shootout victory (2-2 draw; 5-4 penalties) at San Jose Earthquakes on May 24 to qualify for the tournament before tallying a second half brace vs. the New York Red Bulls in a 4-0 quarterfinal victory on July 12 at Toyota Park.* Fire defender Yamith Cuesta has two goals in Open Cup play and is joined by Jalil Anibaba, Diego Chaves and Dominic Oduro as the team's goal scorers in the 2011 competition.UPDATED: MARCO PAPPA AND ORR BAROUCH CALLED FOR INTERNATIONAL DUTYChicago Fire players Marco Pappa and Orr Barouch will represent Guatemala and Israel, respectively, during the international fixture dates next week. Both players will depart Chicago following the Men in Red's Lamar Hunt U.S. Open Cup semifinal tilt against the Richmond Kickers at TOYOTA PARK on Tuesday, Aug. 30.Pappa and Los Chapines will take on St. Vincent/Grenadines in Guatemala City on Friday, Sept. 2 before traveling to Belmopan, Belize to take on Belize on Tuesday, Sept. 6.Barouch will join the Israel U-21 side for an international friendly against the England U-21 side on Monday, Sept. 5.Both players are set to return to TOYOTA PARK in advance of the Fire's next league match against the San Jose Earthquakes at Buck Shaw Stadium on Saturday, Sept. 10 at 9:30 p.m. CT (CSN).CHICAGO FIRE TO CHALLENGE FAMED MEXICAN SIDE CLUB DEPORTIVO GUADALAJARA PRESENTED BY MILLER LITE AT TOYOTA PARK ON SEPTEMBER 14The Chicago Fire Soccer Club will welcome for the first time at TOYOTA PARK the 11-time champions in the Primera DivisiÃ¯Â¿Â½n in Mexican Soccer, Club Deportivo Guadalajara in an international friendly Presented by Miller Lite on Wednesday, Sept. 14 at 8:00 p.m. CT.Advance tickets for the Chicago Fire and Chivas del Guadalajara match are available by calling 888-MLS-Fire or by visiting Chicago-Fire.com.
