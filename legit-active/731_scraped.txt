Recognition: A Special Report
The 1743 Society
The 1743 Society recognizes donors of yearly gifts of $100
through $4,999. The society's name is derived from the year that
the University of Delaware began as a small academy near New
London, Pa., in the home of the distinguished clergyman, the Rev.
Francis Alison. During the 250th anniversary of the founding of
Alison's school, the University's annual giving recognition
society was renamed to reflect the institution's long tradition
of excellence.
Each year, gifts to the University come in the form of
checks, securities, property and gifts-in-kind. Corporate
matching gifts count toward membership in this society. Donors of
$250 or more are recognized in this report.
Recognition in The 1743 Society is accorded at these
levels:
Sustainers    $2,500-4,999
Founders      $1,000-2,499
Fellows           $500-999
Sponsors          $250-499
Members           $100-249
The University encourages and honors all donor requests to
restrict gifts to specific colleges, departments and special programs of
the donor's interest.
To request additional information about The 1743 Society,
please contact the development office at (302) 831-2104.
