He attended many churches in the Manassas area and worked as a handyman. He was a skilled mason, who loved working with his hands, and lives on, in his many projects that still stand. Those that knew Henry will remember him as a kind and fun loving man, who grew up in Manassas Park and made many friends. He played Little League Baseball and football in the GMFL, and loved nature and the outdoors. Henry fished, hunted and enjoyed living life out of doors.He is survived by his brother, William L. Humphries of Manassas; three sisters, Bonnie A. Rowe and her husband Mark of Manassas Park; Frances L. Humphries and her partner John McDonald of Manassas Park; Pamela Phipps and her husband David of Manassas Park; and several nieces and nephews. The family will receive friends on Monday, March 1st, 2010 from 6 to 8 p.m. at the Baker-Post Funeral Home & Cremation Center, 10001 Nokesville Rd. Manassas. A memorial service will begin at 8 p.m. in the funeral home chapel with Pastor Keith Powers officiating.Interment will be held at Buckhall Cemetery at a later date.Memorial contributions may be made to SERVE, 10056 Dean Dr, Manassas, VA 20110.
