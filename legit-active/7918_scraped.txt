sizeVery large text sizeAdvertisement South Africa will replace conflict-hit Libya as hosts of the 2013 Africa Cup of Nations, the organisers said in a statement on Tuesday.Libya, where fighting continues between pro and anti-Moamer Kadhafi forces for control of several cites, take over the 2017 tournament from original hosts South Africa.Algeria and Nigeria were the other contenders for the 2013 Cup of Nations with the West Africans claiming they should be awarded the event having been chosen as stand-by hosts five years ago in case Libya withdrew.The decision was reached after the first half of a two-day Confederation of African Football (CAF) executive committee meeting at the Cairo headquarters of the continental governing body.A brief statement said the swap was necessary because of the "current political situation prevailing in Libya" and added that South Africa would also take over the 2014 African Nations Championship for local-based footballers.South Africa staged and won the 1996 Cup of Nations when Kenya withdrew and after successfully hosting the 2010 World Cup, the country is perfectly positioned to run a successful biennial showcase of African football.Cup of Nations tournament use four stadiums and South Africa have 10 World Cup venues to choose from in Johannesburg (two), Bloemfontein, Cape Town, Durban, Nelspruit, Polokwane, Port Elizabeth, Pretoria and Rustenburg.Gabon and Equatorial Guinea co-host the 2012 Cup of Nations and the edition originally set for 2014 was brought forward with CAF deciding to stage future Cup of Nations in uneven years.CAF awarded Namibia the 2014 African women's championship, Senegal the 2015 African youth championship, Niger the 2015 African junior championship and Madagascar the 2017 African junior
